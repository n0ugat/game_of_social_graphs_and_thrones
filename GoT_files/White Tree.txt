'''White Tree'''{{Mapref|Viewer's Guide}} is a [[Free Folk|wildling]] village located [[beyond the Wall]]. It is a small settlement dominated by a large [[godswood]]. It is in the far north of the continent of [[Westeros]]. It lies in the [[Haunted Forest]], north-west of [[Castle Black]].

==History==
===[[Game of Thrones: Season 2|''Game of Thrones'': Season 2]]===
White Tree is found abandoned by the great ranging under [[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch|Lord Commander]] [[Jeor Mormont]]. All five of the other villages on the journey from [[Castle Black]] to [[Craster's Keep]] are similarly abandoned. [[Craster]] tells Jeor that the wildlings living in villages have gone to join [[Mance Rayder]].<ref>"[[The North Remembers]]"</ref>

===[[Game of Thrones: Season 3|''Game of Thrones'': Season 3]]===
[[Samwell Tarly]] and [[Gilly]] arrive at White Tree as they flee south to the Wall after the [[Mutiny at Craster's Keep]], and decide to take shelter for the night in an abandoned cabin. They are attacked by a [[White Walker (Valar Morghulis)|White Walker]], but Sam manages to kill it with a [[dragonglass]] dagger he found at the [[Fist of the First Men]]. Shocked at the death of the creature, Sam and Gilly flee south again.<ref>"[[Second Sons (episode)|Second Sons]]"</ref>

==In the books==
In the ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'' novels, Whitetree is a small wildling village, consisting of only four rough communal cabins, a sheepfold, and a well. Its name comes from the giant [[weirwood]] that is in its midst. It is found similarly abandoned in ''A Clash of Kings''.
