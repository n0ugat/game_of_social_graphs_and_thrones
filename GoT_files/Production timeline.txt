==Development==
* '''Before November 2005''': [[David Benioff]] and [[D.B. Weiss]] meet [[George R.R. Martin]] to discuss adapting the books for television. They pass a "test" by guessing the identity of [[Jon Snow]]'s mother.<ref>[http://www.westeros.org/GoT/News/Entry/Watch_the_Emmy_Panel Westeros.org]</ref>
* '''March 2006''': Benioff and Weiss pitch ''Game of Thrones'' to [[HBO]] and [[wikipedia:Showtime (TV channel)|Showtime]].<ref>[http://www.westeros.org/GoT/News/Entry/Watch_the_Emmy_Panel Westeros.org]</ref>
* '''16 January 2007''': HBO options ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]''.<ref>[http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117957532.html?categoryid=14&cs=1 Variety article]</ref><ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/2007/01/17/ George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
* '''31 May 2007''': David Benioff, D.B. Weiss and George R.R. Martin appear on the social networking site ''Second Life'' to talk about the potential TV series.<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/2007/05/31/ George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
* '''5 November 2007''': The [[wikipedia:2007-2008 Writer's Guild of America strike|Writer's Guild of America strike]] begins, delaying work on the books series.<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/27951.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
* '''17 December 2007''': George R.R. Martin confirms work on the series remains suspended due to a strike.<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/27951.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
* '''12 February 2008''': The WGA strike ends, allowing work on the script for ''Game of Thrones''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s pilot to resume.
* '''13 June 2008''': George R.R. Martin confirms that a second draft of the pilot script has been submitted to HBO and that the BBC will be co-funding the project (BBC eventually did not get involved).<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/46002.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
* '''9 September 2008''': HBO exercises its option and buys the television rights to ''A Song of Ice and Fire''.<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/51779.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>

==Pilot filming==
*'''24 October 2009''': Day 1 of pilot production. Work begins at [[wikipedia:Doune Castle|Doune Castle]] in Scotland, which is standing in for [[Winterfell]]. Set and costume rehearsals seem to be taking place rather than filming.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/tayside_and_central/8322843.stm BBC News]</ref><ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/10/filming-at-doune-castle/ ]</ref>
*'''26 October 2009''': Day 3 of pilot production. Filming begins. The Winterfell feast is the first scene shot.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/10/day-3-filming-at-doune-castle/ ]</ref> The German band [[wikipedia:Corvus Corax|Corvus Corax]] appear as a troupe of musicians performing at the feast.<ref>[http://asoiaf.westeros.org/index.php?s=&showtopic=39506&view=findpost&p=1955739 Westeros.org forums]</ref>
*'''27 October 2009''': Day 4 of pilot production. The main cast wraps up shooting at Doune Castle.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/10/day-4-filming-at-doune-castle/ ]</ref>
*'''28 October 2009''': Day 5 of pilot production. No filming as the production relocates to Belfast.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/10/day-5-no-filming-today/ ]</ref><ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/117909.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref> Robert Stromberg, fresh from working on ''[[wikipedia:Avatar (2009 film)|Avatar]]'', is confirmed to be working on visual effects for the pilot.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/10/award-winning-vfx-supervisor-working-on-pilot/ ]</ref>
*'''29 October 2009''': Day 6 of pilot production. The prologue scene is filmed at [[wikipedia:Tollymore Forest Park|Tollymore Forest Park]] with extensive fake snow used.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/10/day-6-filming-the-prologue/ ]</ref>
*'''2 November 2009''': Day 10 of pilot production. The scenes where [[Eddard Stark|Eddard]] executes [[Gared]] and [[Robb Stark|Robb]] and [[Jon Snow|Jon]] find the [[Direwolf|direwolves]] in the snow, are filmed at [[wikipedia:Cairncastle|Cairncastle]], [[wikipedia:County Antrim|County Antrim]].<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/day-11-filming-rolls-on-updated/ ]</ref><ref>[http://www.towerofthehand.com/essays/hbo/pilot_locations_gallery.html Tower of the Hand]</ref>
*'''3 November 2009''': Day 11 of pilot production. George R.R. Martin signs copies of his books in a Belfast book store and is joined by many cast members who mingle and talk with fans into the evening.<ref>[http://thewertzone.blogspot.com/2009/11/belfast-report-01.html The Wertzone]</ref><ref>[http://thewertzone.blogspot.com/2009/11/signing-pictures.html The Wertzone]</ref><ref>[http://thewertzone.blogspot.com/2009/11/belfast-report-02.html The Wertzone]</ref><ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/reports-from-grrm-signing/ ]</ref><ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/reports-from-the-grrm-moot/ ]</ref><ref>[http://www.westeros.org/GoT/News/Entry/3608/ Westeros.org]</ref> There are indications that the new scene between [[Ros]], [[Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion]] and [[Jaime Lannister|Jaime]] in a [[King's Landing]] [[Pleasure house|brothel]] was also filmed on this day.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/day-11-filming-rolls-on-updated/ ]</ref>
*'''4 November 2009''': Day 12 of production. The scene in Winterfell courtyard where [[Tommen Baratheon|Tommen]] and [[Bran Stark|Bran]] are sparring is filmed.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/day-13-filming-something/ ]</ref><ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/118374.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
*'''5 November 2009''': Day 13 of production. Filming takes place at [[wikipedia:Castle Ward|Castle Ward]], [[wikipedia:County Down|County Down]].<ref>[http://www.westeros.org/GoT/News/Entry/3624/ Westeros.org]</ref> The scene being shot is most likely King [[Robert Baratheon|Robert]]'s arrival at Winterfell, due to the presence of an effects-enhanced carriage that will be turned into Cersei's wheelhouse.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/day-17-more-filming/ ]</ref>
*'''10 November 2009''': Day 18 of production. Filming has been underway for the last few days at the [[Paint Hall]] in Belfast. The famous, "The things I do for love," scene is apparently shot on this day.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/day-18-final-days-of-uk-filming/ ]</ref>
*'''11 November 2009''': Day 19 of production. It is reported that filming of Season 1 will be spread over 30 weeks, if the series goes ahead.<ref>[http://www.westeros.org/GoT/News/Entry/3637/ Westeros.org]</ref>
*'''12 November 2009''': Day 20 of production. Filming in Northern Ireland is concluded and the production moves to Morocco.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/day-21-filming-in-morocco/ ]</ref>
*'''13 November 2009''': Day 21 of production. Filming in Morocco gets underway at the [[wikipedia:Ouarzazate|Ouarzazate]] studio.
*'''16 November 2009''': Day 24 of production. George R.R. Martin reports arriving at the studio and that the filming of the first meeting between Khal [[Drogo]] and [[Daenerys Targaryen|Daenerys]] has already taken place. The wedding scene is filmed that night.<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/118848.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
*'''18 November 2009''': Day 26 of production. Principle photography of the pilot is concluded.<ref>[http://winter-is-coming.net/2009/11/thats-a-wrap/ ]</ref>
*'''19 November 2009''': The pilot wrap party is held.<ref>[http://grrm.livejournal.com/120749.html George R.R. Martin's blog]</ref>
