'''David John Bradley''' (born 17 April 1942) is an English actor. He is well known for his recurring role as Argus Filch in the [[wikipedia:Harry Potter (film series)|''Harry Potter'']] film series (with [[Natalia Tena]], [[Freddie Stroma]], [[Michelle Fairley]], [[Jim Broadbent]] and [[Bronson Webb]]). He started acting on stage in 1971, and won a [[wikipedia:Laurence Olivier Award|Laurence Olivier Award]] in 1991 for a supporting role in [[wikipedia:King Lear|''King Lear'']]. His more recent credits include [[wikipedia:The Sarah Jane Adventures|''The Sarah Jane Adventures'']], [[wikipedia:Dinosaurs On A Spaceship|''Doctor Who'']], ''[[wikipedia:Ashes to Ashes (TV series)|Ashes to Ashes]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The Tudors|The Tudors]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The Colour of Magic (TV film)|The Colour of Magic]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Broadchurch|Broadchurch]]'' (alongside [[Jacob Anderson]] and [[Susan Brown]]), as well as the films [[wikipedia:Hot Fuzz|''Hot Fuzz'']] (alongside [[Rory McCann]] and [[Jim Broadbent]]) and [[wikipedia:Captain America: The First Avenger|''Captain America: The First Avenger'']] (with [[Natalie Dormer]]).

In ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', he is playing the role of Lord [[Walder Frey]], and was announced in the role on 24 November 2010. With the character heading the [[Red Wedding]] massacre, Bradley himself has stated that he would like to see Walder die a painful death.
