'''''Wonders Made by Man'''''{{Ref|GOT701}} is a book written by [[Lomas Longstrider]], a famous [[Westeros]]i explorer.

==History==
===[[Game of Thrones: Season 7|''Game of Thrones'': Season 7]]===
''[[Legends of the Long Night]]'', the book [[Gilly]] is reading from the [[Citadel]] in [[Oldtown]], refers to Lomas Longstrider and his ''Wonders Made by Man''.{{Ref|GOT701}}

==In the books==
In the ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'' novels, ''Wonders Made by Man'' records nine man-made wonders of the [[Known world|world]], although only eight have been confirmed in-universe so far. These include:
* The [[Wall]]
* The [[Valyrian roads]]
* The [[Titan of Braavos]]
* The triple walls of [[Qarth]]
* The three bells of [[Norvos]]
* The [[Long Bridge]] of [[Volantis]]
* The Palace With a Thousand Rooms in Sarnath
* The Great Pyramid of [[Old Ghis|Ghis]]
There has also been speculation regarding candidates for the ninth wonder. These include:
* The [[Hightower]] of [[Oldtown]]
* [[Harrenhal]]
* The Five Forts, a series of immense fortifications along the northeastern border of [[Yi Ti]]
[[Tyrion Lannister]] is an avid reader of Lomas Longstrider. When he was younger, Tyrion received copies of both of Longstrider's books- ''Wonders Made by Man'' and ''Wonders'' (the latter of which records wonders of the natural world) as gifts from his uncle [[Gerion Lannister|Gerion]], and has memorized numerous passages from them. Gerion liked to set him on the table during feasts and make him recite them. For years afterward, Tyrion cherished a dream that one day he would travel the world and see Longstrider's wonders for himself.
