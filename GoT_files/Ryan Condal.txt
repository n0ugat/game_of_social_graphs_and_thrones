'''Ryan J. Condal''' is an American screenwriter and producer. He serves as showrunner for the prequel ''[[House of the Dragon]]'',<ref name="Hollywood">https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-news/house-of-the-dragon-miguel-sapochnik-leaving-1235208276/</ref> jointly with [[Miguel Sapochnik]] in the [[House of the Dragon: Season 1|first season]].<ref>[https://deadline.com/2019/10/game-of-thrones-new-series-house-of-the-dragons-targaryen-hbo-max-greenlight-1202771886/ ‘Game Of Thrones’ Spinoff ‘House Of The Dragon’ Gets Straight To Series Go-Ahead From HBO,] Deadline, October 29, 2019 - retrieved October 30, 2019</ref> He has previously been a showrunner for the American science fiction drama series ''[[wikipedia:Colony (TV series)|Colony]]''.<ref>[https://ew.com/article/2015/02/03/usa-orders-carlton-cuse-drama-colony-series/ USA orders Carlton Cuse drama 'Colony' to series] Entertainment Weekly, February 3, 2015 - retrieved October 31, 2019</ref> He has also worked on a cancelled fantasy western, at the filming of which he met [[George R.R. Martin]].

Condal is familiar with ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'', having discovered the series when ''A Storm of Swords'' was published. According to Martin, Condal originally wanted to create a show based on the [[Dunk & Egg novellas]], a series that Martin claimed was not ready to be brought to television.<ref>[http://georgerrmartin.com/notablog/2019/10/30/the-dragons-take-wing/ The Dragons Take Wing] George R.R. Martin, Not A Blog, October 30, 2019 - retrieved October 31, 2019</ref> However, Warner Bros. Discovery announced in April 2023 that ''[[A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms]]'', then titled ''A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms: The Hedge Knight'', had been greenlit, and Condal was attached as an executive producer.<ref name="VarietyDaE">[https://variety.com/2023/tv/news/game-of-thrones-prequel-series-a-knight-of-the-seven-kingdoms-hbo-1235578466/ 'Game of Thrones' Prequel Series 'A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms: The Hedge Knight' Ordered at HBO | ''Variety'']</ref>

Condal wrote the pilot script and series bible for ''House of the Dragon''.<ref>[http://georgerrmartin.com/notablog/2019/11/10/a-tip-o-the-crown/ A Tip o' the Crown] George R.R. Martin, Not A Blog, November 10, 2019 - retrieved November 10, 2019</ref>
