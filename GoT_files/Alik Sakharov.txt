'''Alik Sakharov''' (born May 17, 1959) is a Soviet-born American Emmy winning director of photography and television director. Born in Tashkent (in present-day Uzbekistan), he moved to the United States in 1981, becoming an American citizen in 1988.

After directing some short films, documentaries and music videos in the 1980s and 1990s, he started working closely with [[HBO]], serving as a director of photography on ''[[wikipedia:The Sopranos|The Sopranos]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Rome (TV series)|Rome]]'' (for which he won an [[wikipedia:Emmy Award|Emmy Award]]) and ''[[wikipedia:Sex and the City|Sex and the City]]''. He also directed one episode of ''Rome'', as well as some episodes of ''[[wikipedia:Brotherhood (2006 TV series)|Brotherhood]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Easy Money (TV series)|Easy Money]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Rubicon (TV series)|Rubicon]]''.

On ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' Sakharov debuted in the [[Game of Thrones: Season 1|first season]], working as a director of photography on four episodes. For the [[Game of Thrones: Season 2|second season]], he became a full director, a position he retains in both the [[Game of Thrones: Season 3|third]] and [[Game of Thrones: Season 4|fourth]] seasons.<ref>[http://www.westeros.org/GoT/News/Entry/Season_3_Directors/ Westeros.org]</ref>
