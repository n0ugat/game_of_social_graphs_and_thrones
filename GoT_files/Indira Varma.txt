'''Indira Anne Varma''' (born 27 September, 1973)<ref name="bday" /> is an English actress who is best known for her role as [[wikipedia:Niobe of the Voreni|Niobe of the Voreni]] in [[HBO]]'s ''[[wikipedia:Rome_(TV_series)|Rome]]'', which also starred ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' cast members [[Ciarán Hinds]] and [[Tobias Menzies]]. She is also known for her roles in the films ''[[wikipedia:Kama Sutra: A Tale of Love|Kama Sutra: A Tale of Love]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Bride and Prejudice|Bride and Prejudice]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Basic Instinct 2|Basic Instinct 2]]'' as well as the TV series ''[[wikipedia:Luther|Luther]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Human Target|Human Target]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Torchwood|Torchwood]]'', which starred [[Burn Gorman]]. She also voiced Vivienne, a major supporting character, in ''Dragon Age: Inquisition''.

Varma is the only child of a South Asian Indian father and a Swiss mother who was part Genoese Italian. She was a member of Musical Youth Theatre Company. She graduated from the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) in London in 1995.

In ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', Varma plays the role of [[Ellaria Sand]], the paramour of Prince [[Oberyn Martell]]. She joined the cast as a guest star in the [[Game of Thrones: Season 4|fourth]] season, and was promoted to starring cast member in the [[Game of Thrones: Season 5|fifth]] season.
