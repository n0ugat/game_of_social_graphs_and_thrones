'''''House of the Dragon'''''{{HBOref|HOTD}}{{Note|Official translations<br />{{*}} Portuguese: ''A Casa do Drag√£o''<br/>{{*}} Spanish: ''La Casa del Drag√≥n''}} is the second live-action television series of the ''[[World of Westeros]]''. It is the second installment of the franchise overall. It was ordered by [[HBO]] in October 2019. The prequel goes back approximately [[101 AC|200 years]] to center on the beginning of the end of the once all-powerful [[House Targaryen/House of the Dragon|dynasty]] that dominated the [[Seven Kingdoms]] of [[Westeros]].{{Webref|name=Greenlight |author=Dominic Patten, Nellie Andreeva |date=October 29, 2019 |url=https://deadline.com/2019/10/game-of-thrones-new-series-house-of-the-dragons-targaryen-hbo-max-greenlight-1202771886/ |title=‚ÄòGame Of Thrones‚Äô Prequel ‚ÄòHouse Of The Dragon‚Äô Gets Straight To Series Order From HBO |website=Deadline |access-date=December 6, 2023 }}

[[Ryan Condal]] and [[Miguel Sapochnik]] serve as showrunners and executive producers. [[George R.R. Martin]], [[Vince Gerardis]], [[Sara Hess]], and [[Ron Schmidt]] also serve as executive producers, with [[Greg Yaitanes]] as co-executive producer.<ref name="HBO">[https://www.hbo.com/house-of-the-dragon ''House of the Dragon'' | Official Website for the HBO Series | HBO.com]</ref>

The [[House of the Dragon: Season 1|first season]] premiered on August 21, 2022, followed by the [[House of the Dragon: Season 2|second season]] on June 16, 2024. A day earlier, the series was renewed for a [[House of the Dragon: Season 3|third season]], set to begin production in early 2025.

==Production==
The concept for ''House of the Dragon'' was first pitched by [[George R.R. Martin]] in the summer of 2016,{{Webref|name=Dragons |author=George R.R. Martin |date=October 30, 2019 |url=https://georgerrmartin.com/notablog/2019/10/30/the-dragons-take-wing/ |title=The Dragons Take Wing |website=Not A Blog |access-date=October 31, 2019 }} with the working title ''[[The Dance of the Dragons]]''. It was initially helmed by [[Carly Wray]], and then by [[Bryan Cogman]], and finally [[Ryan Condal]] and [[Miguel Sapochnik]].{{Webref|name=THR |author=James Hibberd |date=July 19, 2022 |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-features/house-of-the-dragon-game-of-thrones-prequels-1235181929/ |title=Inside ‚ÄòHouse of the Dragon‚Äô: The Epic Mission to Make the Next ‚ÄòGame of Thrones‚Äô |website=The Hollywood Reporter |access-date=December 6, 2023 }} With the launch of [[HBO Max]] in May 2020, the ''House of the Dragon'' prequel is based on Martin's bestselling ''[[Fire & Blood]]''. The first installment in the prequel book series was published in November 2018. It received a 10-episode straight-to-series order. Miguel Sapochnik directed the pilot and additional episodes.

The title of the show derives from a passage from ''A Game of Thrones'' Chapter 3, Daenerys I: ''The water was scalding hot, but Daenerys did not flinch or cry out. She liked the heat. It made her feel clean. Besides, her brother had often told her that it was never too hot for a Targaryen. "Ours is the house of the dragon," he would say. "The fire is in our blood."

George R.R. Martin has stated that it will take four 10-episode seasons to do the Dance of the Dragons justice,{{Webref|name=Musings |author=George R.R. Martin |date=October 11, 2022 |url=https://georgerrmartin.com/notablog/2022/10/11/random-musings/ |title=Random Musings |website=Not A Blog |access-date=December 6, 2023 }} However, season 2 ran for 8 episodes, compared to season 1's 10-episode run.
