'''Stephen John Dillane''' (born 27 March 1957) is an English actor experienced with roles on both stage and screen. He is arguably best known for his Emmy-nominated performance as [[wikipedia:Thomas Jefferson|Thomas Jefferson]] in the [[HBO]] mini-series ''[[wikipedia:John Adams (miniseries)|John Adams]]''. He has also appeared as [[wikipedia:Leonard Woolf|Leonard Woolf]] in ''[[wikipedia:The Hours|The Hours]]'', [[wikipedia:Merlin|Merlin]] in ''[[wikipedia:King Arthur|King Arthur]]'' and [[wikipedia:Horatio (Hamlet)|Horatio]] in the [[wikipedia:Mel Gibson|Mel Gibson]]-starring version of ''[[wikipedia:Hamlet (1990 film)|Hamlet]]''. In 2001, he appeared in 'The Parole Officer' alongside [[Lena Headey]].

His son, actor Frank Dillane, portrayed the teenage [[wikipedia:Lord Voldemort|Lord Voldemort]] in the film adaptation of ''[[wikipedia:Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)|Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince]]''.

In ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' Dillane played the role of [[Stannis Baratheon]]. He was announced in the role on 19 July 2011 and is part of the starring cast for the [[Game of Thrones: Season 2|second]], [[Game of Thrones: Season 3|third]], [[Game of Thrones: Season 4|fourth]], and [[Game of Thrones: Season 5|fifth]] seasons. Although he plays [[Robert Baratheon]]'s younger brother, Dillane is actually eight years older than [[Mark Addy]], who portrayed Robert.
