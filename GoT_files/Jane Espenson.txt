'''Jane Espenson''' (born 14 July 1964) is an American Hugo-winning scriptwriter and television producer best known for her work on TV series produced by [[wikipedia:Joss Whedon|Joss Whedon]] and [[wikipedia:Ronald D. Moore|Ronald D. Moore]], as well as other projects. She has worked alongside Whedon on ''[http://buffy.wikia.com/wiki/Buffy_the_Vampire_Slayer_and_Angel Buffy the Vampire Slayer]'', ''[http://buffy.wikia.com/wiki/Buffy_the_Vampire_Slayer_and_Angel Angel]'', ''[[wikipedia:Firefly (TV series)|Firefly]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Dollhouse (TV series)|Dollhouse]]'', and Moore on ''[[wikipedia:Star Trek: Deep Space Nine|Star Trek:Deep Space Nine]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)|Battlestar Galactica]]'' and its spin-off ''[[wikipedia:Caprica (TV series)|Caprica]]''. She has also worked on ''[[wikipedia:Gilmore Girls|Gilmore Girls]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The OC|The OC]]'' and is the co-creator of ''[[wikipedia:Warehouse 13|Warehouse 13]]''. She won a [[wikipedia:Hugo Award|Hugo Award]] for her ''Buffy'' episode ''[[wikipedia:Conversations with Dead People|Conversations with Dead People]]''.

Espenson penned the [[A Golden Crown|sixth episode]] of the [[Game of Thrones: Season 1|first season]] of ''[[Game of Thrones]]''. She was unable to return for the [[Game of Thrones: Season 2|second season]] due to working on ''[[wikipedia:Torchwood: Miracle Day|Torchwood: Miracle Day]]'' as a senior writer and producer.

She joined the crew of the ABC series ''Once Upon a Time'' as a co-executive producer and writer for the first season in fall 2011. The show was renewed for a second season and she remains part of the staff, precluding further work on the third season of ''Game of Thrones''.
