'''''A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms'': Season 1''' is the first season of ''[[A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms]]''. It consists of six episodes. It will premiere in 2026 on [[HBO]] and [[HBO Max]]. It is based on ''[[The Hedge Knight (novella)|The Hedge Knight]]'', the first of the [[Dunk & Egg novellas]] by [[George R.R. Martin]].

===Writers===
According to the initial announcement by Warner Bros. Discovery, George R.R. Martin is credited as a writer on the first season.<ref name="VarietyDaE" /> His last writing credit in an adaptation of ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'' was "[[The Lion and the Rose]]," the second episode of the [[Game of Thrones: Season 4|fourth season]] of ''[[Game of Thrones]]''.

In May 2023, writing on the first season was placed on hold due to a {{WPS|2023 Writers Guild of America strike|strike action by the Writers Guild of America}}.<ref>[https://georgerrmartin.com/notablog/2023/05/07/strike/ STRIKE! | George R.R. Martin, ''Not a Blog'']</Ref> The strike ended on September 27, allowing work on the new series to continue.<ref>[https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/writers-guild-strike-end-1235600992/amp/ Writers Guild Strike to End Wednesday: Leadership Votes to Conclude Historic Work Stoppage] - ''The Hollywood Reporter'', Sep 26 2023</ref>

==Production==
Filming on Season 1 started on June 14, 2024<ref name="Wrap" /> in Belfast, Northern Ireland<ref name="Belfast" /> and wrapped on September 13 the same year. Production reportedly went smoothly and without complications.<ref name="Wrap" />

After a visit to the set in Northern Ireland, [[George R.R. Martin]] praised the cast and the showrunner [[Ira Parker]].<ref name="Wrap" />

==In the books==
The season is adapted from ''The Hedge Knight'', the first of the [[Dunk & Egg novellas]].<ref name="VarietyDaE" />
