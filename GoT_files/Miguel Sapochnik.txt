|twitter = http://www.twitter.com/MigSapochnik|dateofbirth = July 1974}}'''Miguel Sapochnik''', born '''Miguel Vicente Rosenberg-Sapochnik''', is an English director of film and television and former storyboard artist. He is best known as the director of the feature film ''[[wikipedia:Repo Men|Repo Men]]''. His most notable television credits are ''[[wikipedia:House M.D. (TV Series)|House M.D.]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Fringe (TV Series)|Fringe]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Awake (TV Series)|Awake]]'', and ''[[wikipedia:Mind Games (TV Series)|Mind Games]]''.

On July 15, 2014, it was announced that Sapochnik would be directing two episodes in the [[Game of Thrones: Season 5|fifth season]], episodes [[The Gift|seven]] and [[Hardhome (episode)|eight]].<ref>[http://winteriscoming.net/2014/07/15/directors-game-thrones-season-5-announced/]</ref> On June 4, 2015, Sapochnik revealed he would be back to direct for the [[Game of Thrones: Season 6|sixth season]], the [[Battle of the Bastards (episode)|ninth]] and the [[The Winds of Winter|tenth]] episodes.<ref>[http://www.ew.com/article/2015/06/23/game-thrones-season-6-directors]</ref>The episodes that Sapochnik has directed have been some of the best received and highest rated during the show's run.
[[File:Miguel Sapochnik.png|thumb|250px|Miguel Sapochnik filming in Season 5.]]

He is an executive producer on ''[[House of the Dragon]]''. In the [[House of the Dragon: Season 1|first season]], he was a director and served as co-showrunner with [[Ryan Condal]],<ref>[https://deadline.com/2019/10/game-of-thrones-new-series-house-of-the-dragons-targaryen-hbo-max-greenlight-1202771886/ ‘Game Of Thrones’ Spinoff ‘House Of The Dragon’ Gets Straight To Series Go-Ahead From HBO,] Deadline, October 30, 2019</ref> but after a falling out between the two, HBO executive Casey Bloys moved Sapochnik to another development deal.<ref>https://variety.com/2023/tv/features/last-of-us-white-lotus-casey-bloys-hbo-1235527633/</ref> His place as director was filled by [[Alan Taylor]].<ref name="Hollywood">https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-news/house-of-the-dragon-miguel-sapochnik-leaving-1235208276/</ref> According to a report by ''Puck'', this followed a standoff in which HBO refused to let his wife [[Alexis Raben]] continue as producer for the [[House of the Dragon: Season 2|second season]].<ref>https://tvline.com/2023/01/03/miguel-sapochnik-wife-house-of-the-dragon-season-2-controversy/</ref>
