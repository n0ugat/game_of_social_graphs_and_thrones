'''Ian McNeice''' (born 2 October 1950) is a British actor. He first came to attention as Harcourt in the iconic 1980s BBC miniseries ''[[wikipedia:Edge of Darkness|Edge of Darkness]]''. Amongst his many other roles are the Baron [[wikipedia:Vladimir Harkonnen|Vladimir Harkonnen]] in the miniseries ''[[wikipedia:Frank Herbert's Dune|Dune]]'' and its sequel, ''[[wikipedia:Frank Herbert's Children of Dune|Children of Dune]]'', the newsreader/town crier in [[HBO]]'s ''[[wikipedia:Rome (TV series)|Rome]]'' and as a doctor in ''[[wikipedia:From Hell (film)|From Hell]]''. He has also played the role of [[wikipedia:Winston Churchill|Winston Churchill]] several times, including three times during the 2010 season of ''[[wikipedia:Doctor Who|Doctor Who]]''.

In ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', McNeice played the role of [[Illyrio Mopatis]] in the unscreened [[Pilot (Game of Thrones)|pilot]]. He was confirmed in the role on 20 October 2009. On 16 September 2010 it was confirmed that McNeice could not reprise the role in the series itself due to a scheduling conflict that could not be resolved. He was replaced by [[Roger Allam]].
