|Last =[[No One]] }}'''Clive Russell''' (born December 7, 1945 in Reeth, England, UK) is an actor. He was raised in Fife, Scotland and oftens performs with a notable Scottish accent.

He played Ser [[Brynden Tully]] in ''[[Game of Thrones]]''. He was announced in the role on June 30, 2012. He debuted in [[Game of Thrones: Season 3|Season 3]], and returned for [[Game of Thrones: Season 6|Season 6]].<ref>[http://watchersonthewall.com/the-season-6-return-of-a-character-confirmed Watchers on the Wall — The season 6 return of a character confirmed!]</ref>

Russell's most notable genre credits include playing the villainous Mr. Vandemar in Neil Gaiman's ''Neverwhere'' and intimidating comic company owner Damien Knox in the Simon Pegg and Jessica Stevenson comedy series ''Spaced''.

He has appeared in films including ''The 13th Warrior'', ''King Arthur'', ''The Wolfman'', ''Sherlock Holmes'' and ''Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows'' and most recently plays a world weary priest in movie ''Fighters Ballad''. He has also appeared in ''Casualty'', ''Coronation Street'' and ''Cracker'' (along with fellow ''Thrones'' actors [[Nicholas Blane]], [[Liam Cunningham]] and [[Ron Donachie]], as well Colin Tierney, the husband of [[Indira Varma]]). He is also appearing in the upcoming movie ''Mary's Ride'' with former ''Game of Thrones'' guest star [[Ian Hanmore]].
