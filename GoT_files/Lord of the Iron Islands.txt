The '''Lord of the Iron Islands'''{{Ref|GOT107}}{{Note|Feminine: '''Lady of the Iron Islands'''{{Scriptref|GOT806}}}} is a title in the [[Seven Kingdoms]] for the ruler of the administrative region of the [[Iron Islands]] under the authority of the [[King of the Andals and the Rhoynar and the First Men|King of the Andals and the First Men]]. It is currently held by [[Yara Greyjoy]] as the Lady of the Iron Islands.

==History==
===Background===
Before [[Aegon's Conquest]], the Iron Islands were originally a sovereign nation. The [[Ironborn]] also controlled the [[Riverlands]], which they had conquered in turn from the [[Stormlands]]. The title was first created after the Ironborn became vassals of [[House Targaryen]]. [[House Greyjoy]] have been the Lords of the Iron Islands for hundreds of years.

===[[Game of Thrones: Season 2|''Game of Thrones'': Season 2]]===
After naming himself King of the Iron Islands, [[Balon Greyjoy|King Balon Greyjoy]] invaded the [[North]] by sending his daughter [[Yara Greyjoy|Yara]] to seize [[Deepwood Motte]], his brother Victarion Greyjoy to seize [[Moat Cailin]] and his only living son [[Theon Greyjoy|Theon]] to raid the [[Stony Shore]].<ref>[[The Ghost of Harrenhal]]</ref> Despite Theon being King Balon's only son, Balon has named his daughter Yara as his heir.<ref>[[What Is Dead May Never Die]]</ref>

==Known Lords of the Iron Islands==
* ~1 AC: {[[Vickon Greyjoy]]}, first Lord of the Iron Islands.
* ? AC: {[[Dalton Greyjoy]]}, Lord of the Iron Islands during the [[Dance of the Dragons]].
* ?–280 AC: {[[Quellon Greyjoy]]}, during the reign of [[Aerys II Targaryen]]. Died during a [[battle at the Mander]].
* 280–289 AC: {[[Balon Greyjoy]]}, firstborn son of Quellon.
''Vacant'' 289 AC: Balon Greyjoy reigns as [[King of the Iron Islands]].
* 289–299 AC: Balon Greyjoy, reinstated as Lord of the Iron Islands after his failed rebellion.
''Vacant'' 299–305 AC: Balon Greyjoy and {[[Euron Greyjoy]]} both reign using the title [[King of the Iron Islands]].
* 305 AC–present: [[Yara Greyjoy]], daughter of Balon.

==In the books==
The Iron Islands is one of the two constituent regions of the [[Seven Kingdoms]], alongside [[Dorne]], whose rulers aren't "Lords Paramount". While the rulers of Dorne hold the title of "Prince", the rulers of the Iron Islands are merely lords.
