'''Sean Bean''' (born '''Shaun Mark Bean''', 17 April 1959) is an English actor who has worked in film, television and theatrical productions, as well as providing voice-overs for commercials and computer games.

He first became well known for his portrayal of [[wikipedia:Richard Sharpe (fictional character)|Richard Sharpe]] in the long-running British television series [[wikipedia:Sharpe (TV series)|''Sharpe'']], before appearing as [[wikipedia:Boromir|Boromir]] in [[wikipedia:Peter Jackson|Peter Jackson]]'s [[wikipedia:Lord of the Rings film trilogy|''Lord of the Rings'']] movie trilogy. He has also carved out a role as a [[wikipedia:Hollywood|Hollywood]] villain, appearing in antagonistic roles in films such as [[wikipedia:Patriot Games (film)|''Patriot Games'']], [[wikipedia:GoldenEye|''GoldenEye'']] and [[wikipedia:National Treasure (film)|''National Treasure'']]. Other notable works include [[wikipedia:Odysseus|Odysseus]] in ''[[wikipedia:Troy (film)|Troy]]'' (which also featured [[James Cosmo]] and [[Julian Glover]]), Dr. Merrick in ''[[wikipedia:The Island (2005 film)|The Island]]'', Zeus in ''[[wikipedia:Percy Jackson & the Olympians: The Lightning Thief|Percy Jackson &amp; the Olympians: The Lightning Thief]]'' and Ulric in ''[[wikipedia:Black Death (film)|Black Death]]'', which also starred ''Game of Thrones'' cast members [[Emun Elliott]] and [[Carice van Houten]].

He starred in [[HBO]]'s ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' as Lord [[Eddard Stark]]. He was announced in the role on 19 July 2009. He appeared in nine episodes of the [[Game of Thrones: Season 1|first season]] (though was credited for all ten) before being written out, following the character's fate as depicted in the novel.

In an interview after [[Game of Thrones: Season 4|Season 4]] aired, Bean mentioned that he would enjoy possibly returning to ''Game of Thrones'' to play Ned Stark in flashback scenes.<ref>"[http://winteriscoming.net/2014/07/18/sean-bean-wants-return-game-thrones/ Sean Bean wants to return to Game of Thrones]</ref>
