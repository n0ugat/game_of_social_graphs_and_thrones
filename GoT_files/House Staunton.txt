'''House Staunton'''{{Ref|HOTD110}}{{Note|Gerardys mentions a declaration from Staunton.}} of [[Rook's Rest]] is a noble house from the [[Crownlands]]. Their heraldry displays a black pair of wings with a gold crown on a white field, beneath a black and white checkered chief.<ref name="BTS" />

==History==
===[[House of the Dragon: Season 1|''House of the Dragon'': Season 1]]===
[[Man-at-arms|Men-at-arms]] from House Staunton take part in the [[War for the Stepstones]].{{Ref|HOTD103}}

Lord [[Simon Staunton]] declares for [[Rhaenyra Targaryen|Rhaenyra]] at the start of the [[Dance of the Dragons]] and is named to the [[Black Council]].{{Ref|HOTD110}}

=== [[House of the Dragon: Season 2|House of the Dragon Season 2]] ===
When [[Baela Targaryen|Baela]] informs the Council she witnessed [[Criston Cole]] marching a Green army through the Crownlands, Simon fears that Cole may target Rook's Rest on his march to confront Prince [[Daemon Targaryen|Daemon]] at [[Harrenhal]] and requests Rhaenyra's leave to depart Dragonstone so he may return to his seat and fortify it.<ref>''[[House of the Dragon]]'': [[House of the Dragon: Season 2|Season 2]], Episode 3: "[[The Burning Mill]]" (2024).</ref>

Simon commands the garrison of Rook's Rest when Criston Cole's Green army [[Battle at Rook's Rest|besiege]] it, and witness the aerial dragon battle between [[Meleys]], [[Sunfyre]], and [[Vhagar]]. Rook's Rest is ultimately captured.<ref>''[[House of the Dragon]]'': [[House of the Dragon: Season 2|Season 2]], Episode 4: "[[The Red Dragon and the Gold]]" (2024)</ref>

==Historical members==
*Lord [[Simon Staunton]], head of the house during the [[Dance of the Dragons]].

==In the books==
In the ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'' novels, House Staunton's [[House words|words]] and sigil are unknown, though according to semi-canon sources the latter is two black wings upon a white fess on checkered black and grey.

During [[Jaehaerys Targaryen's uprising]], Lord Staunton remained loyal to [[Maegor Targaryen|King Maegor]] but eventually surrendered the [[Red Keep]] to [[Jaehaerys I Targaryen|Jaehaerys]]. He, along with Lord Darklyn and Jordan Towers, was imprisoned in the [[Black Cells|black cells]] and later pardoned by Jaehaerys. Lord Staunton spoke with the king when the latter resided at [[Dragonstone]].

Lord Staunton's daughter Cassella was murdered by Androw Farman at Dragonstone in 54 AC. Five years later, Lord Staunton died of the Shivers along with his wife and children.

Lord Symond Staunton was [[Aerys II Targaryen]]'s [[Master of Laws|master of laws]]. He attempted to outlaw [[Tournament|tourneys]].
