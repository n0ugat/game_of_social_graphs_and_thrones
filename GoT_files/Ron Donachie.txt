'''Ronald Eaglesham Porter''' (born 26 April 1956), known professionally as '''Ron Donachie''', is a Scottish actor. He has an immense list of acting roles to his name, including many UK television dramas, including ''[[wikipedia:Doctor Who|Doctor Who]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Rebus (TV series)|Rebus]]'', and ''[[wikipedia:The Bill|The Bill]]''. In film he may be best known for a supporting role in [[wikipedia:James Cameron|James Cameron]]'s ''[[wikipedia:Titanic (film)|Titanic]]'', where he played a character that was also called "Master-at-Arms".

In ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', Donachie played [[Knighthood|Ser]] [[Rodrik Cassel]]. He was confirmed in the role on 20 October 2009. He initially appeared as a guest star in the [[Game of Thrones: Season 1|first season]] and returned in this role for the [[Game of Thrones: Season 2|second season]].

Donachie had read the ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'' novels and other works by Martin before working on ''Game of Thrones''; [[Michelle Fairley]] described him as "an intellect".<ref name="wheeler">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vLkvLpPWWv Game of Thrones with George R.R. Martin and Michelle Fairley]. Wheeler Centre, 2015-01-26.</ref> He is the father of [[Daniel Portman]], who plays [[Podrick Payne]].<ref>[http://www.makinggameofthrones.com/production-diary/50-things-season-2-pt-2 Making Game of Thrones blog]</ref>
