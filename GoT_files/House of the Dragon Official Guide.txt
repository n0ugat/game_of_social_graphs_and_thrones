==Character Guide==
===Major houses===
====House Targaryen====
=====Jaehaerys I Targaryen=====
''King Jaehaerys, known commonly as the Old King, reigned over the realm for nearly six decades of peace and prosperity. Father to thirteen children, unfortunate tragedy claimed both his sons and heirs, leaving the question of his succession as a cause for unrest. Calling upon a Great Council to choose his heir peacefully, his grandson Viserys Targaryen was named his successor.''{{Guideref|Jaehaerys I Targaryen}}

======Personal Item: Jaehaerys I's Crown======
''Jaehaerys I Targaryen, the fourth king of the Targaryen dynasty, was the longest-reigning king in the history of the Seven Kingdoms. His crown of yellow gold is a symbol of his reign and in turn a representation of harmony in the realm.''{{Guideref|Jaehaerys I Targaryen}}

=====Rhaenys Targaryen=====
''The granddaughter of King Jaehaerys I Targaryen, Princess Rhaenys was once a possible contender for the Iron Throne. However, the lords of Westeros overlooked her claim in favor of her male first cousin, Viserys. She later wed the powerful Lord Corlys Velaryon, and mothered two children, Laena and Laenor. Whispers in the kingdom call her "The Queen Who Never Was." During Aegon's corronation,{{Sic|coronation}} Rhaenys escaped on Meleys and shared the news of Viserys' passing with Rhaenys and Daemon.''

=====Aemma Arryn=====
''Wife to King Viserys I Targaryen and mother of Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen, Queen Aemma passed away in childbirth after delivering a boy. The young male heir, Prince Baelon, also died shortly thereafter. Her family is the principal house in the Vale, where they reside in the Eyrie.''

=====Viserys I Targaryen=====
''The grandson of Old King Jaehaerys, Viserys Targaryen was ultimately chosen by the lords of Westeros over his cousin Princess Rhaenys Targaryen to rule. He was the fifth ruler of the Targaryen dynasty.''

''A level-headed decision maker, his reign was peaceful, though not without its challenges. He had one daughter, Princess Rhaenyra Targaryen, with Queen Aemma Arryn.''

''Upon Aemma's death, Viserys married Alicent Hightower. The two had several children, the oldest of whom was a son, Aegon Targaryen. Although he had named Rhaenyra his heir, the birth of Aegon created conflict over the question of Viserys' succession.''

''After many years of declining health, he died in his chambers, unable to broker a peace between his family members prior to his passing.''

======Personal Item: Blackfyre======

Its legend lived on and it was passed through the hands of a great deal of Targaryen kings, seeing many battles. Blackfyre is a treasured ancestral blade of House Targaryen.''

=====Rhaenyra Targaryen=====

''To strengthen their family's claim, Rhaenyra married her uncle Daemon. Together they have two children of their own, Aegon and Viserys.''

Prior to Rhaenyra's marriage to Laenor Velaryon, she had complicated relationships with both her uncle Daemon and her sworn protector Criston Cole.''

====House Velaryon====
=====Vaemond Velaryon=====
''Vaemond Velaryon was the younger brother to Lord Corlys Velaryon, the Sea Snake, and a commander in the Velaryon navy. His ancestral home was Driftmark and he was proud of his lineage. In an attempt to lay claim to the Driftmark throne,{{Sic|Driftwood Throne}} he sought a petition with the king to delegitimize Lucerys Velaryon's succession. This slander towards the royal family was deemed treason and he was put to death for his crimes.''

======Personal Items: Velaryon Shield and Armor======
''House Velaryon rules over the island of Driftmark, the largest island in Blackwater Bay. Their coat of arms is a silver seahorse on a sea green field. Vaemond Velaryon bore this sigil proudly on his shield and armor, as the house's naval fleet fought the Triarchy in the War for the Stepstones.''

======Personal Item: Velaryon Funeral Garb======

=====Corlys Velaryon "The Sea Snake"=====
''Lord Corlys Velaryon is the leader of a formidable family with ancient roots. He is married to Princess Rhaenys Targaryen, who was passed over in favor of Viserys to rule. Nicknamed "the Sea Snake" in his youth, Lord Corlys was a great explorer and is now the richest man in Westeros. His vast wealth includes the largest navy in the world, which puts him in a powerful position at court. He is Master of Driftmark, an island west of Dragonstone in Blackwater Bay, that is the seat of House Velaryon. A shrewd man, the Sea Snake often disagreed with the king about his approach to the realm, but remained loyal to the crown.''

''When King Viserys died, Corlys placed blood over power, supporting Princess Rhaenyra's claim to the Iron Throne.''

======Personal Item: Master of Ships======
