'''Peter Vaughan''' (born '''Peter Ewart Ohm'''; 4 April 1923 – 6 December 2016) was a British actor. He has appeared many times on screen and on stage since the 1950s, including a leading role alongside Frank Sinatra in ''The Naked Runner''. He appeared in many British television series of the 1960s and 1970s, including ''[[wikipedia:Porridge (TV series)|Porridge]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Randall and Hopkirk (Deceased)|Randall and Hopkirk (Deceased)]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Citizen Smith|Citizen Smith]]''. He also played the role of [[wikipedia:Denethor|Denethor]] in the acclaimed 1981 BBC radio dramatization of ''[[wikipedia:The Lord of the Rings|The Lord of the Rings]]''. More recent roles included a recurring stint on ''[[wikipedia:Lark Rise to Candleford (TV series)|Lark Rise to Candleford]]''.

In ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' Vaughan played [[Order of Maesters|Maester]] [[Aemon]]. He was confirmed in the role on 16 July 2010.

Vaughan was partially blind and portrayed the blind character, Aemon, in the show.

Peter Vaughan passed away on 6 December 2016 due to natural causes at the age of 93 - less than two years after his filming for ''Game of Thrones'' ended, when his character died within the story from old age.<ref>[http://watchersonthewall.com/peter-vaughan-known-many-game-thrones-maester-aemon-passed-away/#more-83590 ]</ref>
