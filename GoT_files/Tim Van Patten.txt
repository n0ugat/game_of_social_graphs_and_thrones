'''Timothy "Tim" Van Patten''' (born 10 June 1959) is an Emmy-winning American actor, scriptwriter, producer and director. He is best known for his directing work for [[HBO]], having directed episodes of ''[[w:c:boardwalkempire|Boardwalk Empire]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The Pacific (miniseries)|The Pacific]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Rome (TV series)|Rome]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Deadwood (TV series)|Deadwood]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The Wire (TV series)|The Wire]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The Sopranos|The Sopranos]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Sex and the City|Sex and the City]]''. He has also directed episodes of ''[[wikipedia:Ed (TV series)|Ed]]'' and ''[[wikipedia:Touched by an Angel|Touched by an Angel]]''. Patten's most notable project was ''The Sopranos'', for which he directed twenty episodes and won a [[wikipedia:Writers Guild Award|Writer's Guild Award]] and an [[wikipedia:Edgar Award|Edgar Award]]. Another episode he wrote won an [[wikipedia:Emmy Award|Emmy Award]] for Best Writing.

Patten directed the first two episodes of ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', including reshoots of the [[Pilot (Game of Thrones)|pilot]] originally directed by [[Thomas McCarthy]]. He was nominated for a [[wikipedia:Emmy Award|Emmy Award]] for his work on the [[Winter Is Coming|first episode]] of the series.

Van Patten did not return as a director for the [[Game of Thrones: Season 2|second season]]. He did attend the 2012 Golden Globes ceremony and celebrated [[Peter Dinklage]] winning Best Supporting Actor.
